椭圆曲线的定义

[Woldram MathWorld](Elliptic Curve -- from Wolfram MathWorld)：

Informally, an elliptic curve is a type of cubic curve whose solutions are confined to a region of space that is topologically equivalent to a torus. The Weierstrass elliptic function ℘(z;g2,g3)℘(z;g_2,g_3)℘(z;g2​,g3​) describes how to get from this torus to the algebraic form of an elliptic curve.

Formally,an elliptic curve over a field KKK is a non singular cubic curve in two variables, f(X,Y)=0f(X,Y) = 0f(X,Y)=0 ,with a KKK-rational point(which may be a point at infinity). The field KKK is usually taken to the complex numbers C\mathbb{C}C,reals R\mathbb{R}R,rationals Q\mathbb{Q}Q,algebraic extensions of Q\mathbb{Q}Q, p-adic numbers Qp\mathbb{Q_p}Qp​, or a finite field
